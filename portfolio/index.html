CUHK Jockey Club AI For the Future Project

EdTech Curriculum Design GAN Style Transfer
The Challenge

Bridging the gap between advanced academic AI research and secondary education. The goal was to translate complex algorithms (Style Transfer & GANs) into an accessible, interactive curriculum for high school students without diluting the technical essence.

The Solution
  • From Theory to Practice: Led a cross-functional team to translate research into a standardized curriculum.
  • Interactive Learning: Developed Gradio-based applications that allow students to experiment with AI models (zi2zi, mxfont) using real historical data (e.g., Orchid Pavilion).
  • Comprehensive Resources: Delivered the entire content package, from bilingual manuscripts to instructor guidebooks.
Impact

Secured 24 strategic schools (comprising Champion and Network tiers) for the Phase 2 rollout. The module was officially launched at the Learning & Teaching Expo 2025 in July, establishing a benchmark for successful Knowledge Transfer.

Museum AI Audio Guide

GPT-SoVITS Gemini Flash Voice Cloning Prompt Engineering
The Problem

Traditional audio guide production is labor-intensive and slow. The museum faced tight exhibition deadlines, making it impossible to cover the desired 62 exhibits using standard recording methods.

Methodology
  • Script Generation: Engineered advanced prompt strategies using Gemini 2.5 Flash to generate culturally accurate, engaging scripts.
  • Voice Cloning: Fine-tuned GPT-SoVITS models using self-preprocessed audio data, achieving high-fidelity voice cloning for historical narration.
  • Pipeline Engineering: Automating the flow from raw text to trilingual audio generation.
Impact

Expanded content from 20 to 62 exhibits in just 2 weeks (300% boost). Delivered the museum's first AI guide.

Project Manager & Data Strategist

Sheng Xuanhuai Archives Digitization

Data Strategy Crowdsourcing OCR/HTR Digital Humanities
The Challenge

Identified a critical data mismatch: The algorithms were initially trained on synthetic computer fonts, which failed to capture the complexity of natural handwriting found in historical archives. While the Art Museum had digitized and transcribed the Sheng Xuanhuai archives, the dataset lacked the character-level coordinates (visual-text alignment) essential.

Methodology
  • Strategic Alliance: Partnered with the School of History and Civilization at SNNU, co-developing a specialized annotation platform.
  • Workflow & Execution: Designed the annotation process, recruited volunteers, and executed the mapping of character-level coordinates (bounding boxes) based on expert transcriptions.
Impact

Delivered a foundational dataset of 4 million character-level annotations, resolving the domain gap and unlocking the development of core OCR/HTR/Calligraphy generation algorithms.
